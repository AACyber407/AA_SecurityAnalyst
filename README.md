# About Me as a Network Engineer

I am an entry-level network engineer with a growing foundation in both networking and cybersecurity, backed by three certifications: CCNA, CompTIA Network+, and CompTIA Security+. I am currently working in an entry-level networking/IT role, where I apply these skills in a live environment, while also using personal projects to explore designs and scenarios beyond my day-to-day work.

My strongest area is network design, routing, and switching. I am comfortable working with VLAN segmentation, trunking, inter-VLAN routing, Spanning Tree Protocol, and gateway redundancy protocols like HSRP. These are not just concepts I have studied, I have applied them directly in my Two-Campus Enterprise Network project, where I designed a fully redundant, VPN-connected infrastructure spanning two physical sites.

Alongside networking, I bring an entry-level cybersecurity mindset to my work. I understand how to apply basic security controls, like ACLs and VLAN segmentation, not just for network organization, but as a first layer of defense. I am continuing to build on this through my Security+ studies and ongoing IT coursework.

I am currently majoring in IT, and my goal is to keep growing toward a role that blends network engineering with security, designing infrastructure that is not just functional, but resilient and defensible from the ground up.
